Dinosaurs

Triceratops: Meet the three-horned face! Triceratops was a bulky herbivore from the Late Cretaceous, famous for its impressive frill and three prominent facial horns. These features likely played a role in defense against predators like Tyrannosaurus rex and in social display. With its sturdy build, Triceratops was a formidable plant-eater.

Stegosaurus: Easily recognized by the distinctive row of bony plates running along its back and the spiked tail, Stegosaurus was a large, herbivorous dinosaur of the Late Jurassic period. These plates likely served for display and defense, while its thagomizer (the spiky tail) could deliver a powerful blow to any would-be attacker.

Tyrannosaurus Rex

Imagine a colossal predator, the undisputed king of the Late Cretaceous! With its massive jaws packed with serrated teeth and powerful hind legs, Tyrannosaurus rex was a fearsome hunter. Though its tiny arms might look comical, this iconic dinosaur was a force to be reckoned with, dominating its ecosystem.
